## Questions to Answer

### Idea/Draft Phase

These are the questions you can answer **NOW** when planning your podcast episode. Focus on the core content and format decisions.

#### Episode Basics
- **Episode title**: What's the episode called?
- **Episode number**: What number is this episode? (if part of a series)
- **Topic**: What's the main subject?
- **Angle**: What's your unique take or approach?
- **Target length**: Short (10-15 min), medium (20-30 min), long (45-60 min+)?

#### Format & Structure
- **Format**: Interview, solo, co-hosted, panel, narrative storytelling?
- **Segments**: What are the main segments? (intro, main content, Q&A, outro, ads, etc.)
- **Tone**: Conversational, educational, entertaining, serious, humorous?
- **Pacing**: Fast-paced, relaxed, contemplative?

#### Content Planning
- **Key points**: What are the 3-5 main points to cover?
- **Stories/examples**: What stories or examples will you use?
- **Guests**: Who will be on the episode? (if applicable)
- **Questions**: What questions will you ask? (for interviews)

#### Audio Elements
- **Music/sound**: Intro music, outro music, sound effects?
- **Transitions**: How will segments transition?

---

### Publishing Phase

These are questions you figure out **LATER** - after recording and during production/distribution.

#### Production Details
- **Show notes**: What should go in the show notes?
- **Timestamps**: Key moments to timestamp?
- **Links**: Resources, references, or links to include?
- **Transcript**: Will there be a transcript?
- **Audio editing**: What editing is needed? (remove filler words, add music, etc.)

#### Distribution
- **Podcast feed**: Which podcast feed/show?
- **Release date**: When will this be published?
- **Social media**: How will this be promoted? (Twitter, LinkedIn, Instagram, TikTok clips?)
- **Episode description**: SEO-optimized description for podcast platforms?
- **Tags/categories**: Apple Podcasts categories, tags?
- **Audiogram/clips**: Create short clips for social media?

#### Guest Coordination (if applicable)
- **Guest approval**: Does guest need to approve before publishing?
- **Guest promotion**: How will guest promote the episode?
- **Guest materials**: Bio, headshot, links for show notes?

## Available Approaches

Choose the approach that best fits your podcast format and production goals.

### Approach 1: Quick Solo Episode

**When to use**:
- Short episode (10-20 minutes)
- Solo host, no guests
- Timely topic or quick update
- Minimal production needed
- Personal or conversational podcast

**Strategy**:
- Light scripting - outline with bullet points, not full script
- Record in one take (or minimal editing)
- Simple intro/outro music
- Quick turnaround from recording to publishing

**Output**: Single podcast episode, 10-20 minutes

**Workflow**:
1. Answer core Idea/Draft questions (title, topic, key points, length)
2. Create bullet-point outline (not full script)
3. Record episode in one session
4. Light editing (remove major mistakes, add music)
5. Answer Publishing phase questions
6. Write show notes
7. Publish

**Timeline**: 1-2 days from idea to published

**Script Format**: Bullet-point outline with key talking points

---

### Approach 2: Structured Interview Episode

**When to use**:
- Interview format with guest
- Medium to long episode (30-60 minutes)
- Professional or educational podcast
- Want polished, high-quality result
- Guest requires coordination

**Strategy**:
- Prepare detailed questions and research guest
- Coordinate with guest (scheduling, tech check, pre-interview)
- Record with structured segments
- Professional editing and production
- Comprehensive show notes with timestamps

**Output**: Single interview episode, 30-60 minutes

**Workflow**:
1. Answer all Idea/Draft phase questions
2. Research guest and prepare questions
3. Coordinate with guest:
   - Schedule recording
   - Tech check (audio quality, software)
   - Pre-interview discussion (topics, boundaries)
4. Create interview script with segments:
   - Intro (introduce guest and topic)
   - Main interview (prepared questions + follow-ups)
   - Rapid-fire or fun segment (optional)
   - Outro (thank guest, where to find them)
5. Record episode
6. Edit audio:
   - Remove filler words and long pauses
   - Add intro/outro music
   - Balance audio levels
   - Add transitions
7. Get guest approval (if required)
8. Answer Publishing phase questions
9. Create show notes with timestamps
10. Create audiogram clips for social media
11. Coordinate promotion with guest
12. Publish

**Timeline**: 1-2 weeks from booking guest to published

**Script Format**: Detailed question list with intro/outro scripts

---

### Approach 3: Narrative Series

**When to use**:
- Multi-episode story or investigation
- Highly produced, scripted content
- Complex topic that needs multiple episodes
- Want cinematic, immersive experience
- Documentary or storytelling style

**Strategy**:
- Plan entire series arc before recording
- Write full scripts for each episode
- Record narration, interviews, and ambient sound
- Extensive editing and sound design
- Release episodes on schedule (weekly, bi-weekly)

**Output**: Series of 3-8 episodes with narrative arc

**Workflow**:
1. Answer Idea/Draft questions for overall series theme
2. Break story into episode structure:
   - Episode 1: Hook and setup
   - Episodes 2-N: Development and investigation
   - Final episode: Resolution and conclusion
3. For each episode:
   - Write full script with narration
   - Identify interview subjects
   - Plan sound design (music, effects, ambient sound)
4. Record all narration
5. Conduct and record interviews
6. Gather ambient sound and music
7. Edit each episode:
   - Assemble narrative structure
   - Mix narration, interviews, and sound
   - Add music and effects
   - Balance audio levels
8. Create series trailer
9. Answer Publishing phase questions for each episode
10. Create comprehensive show notes for each episode
11. Plan release schedule
12. Create promotional materials (audiograms, social media posts)
13. Publish series

**Timeline**: 2-3 months from concept to full series published

**Script Format**: Full scripts with narration, interview clips, and sound design notes

**Series Structure Example**:
```
Series: "The Rise of Remote Work" (6 episodes)
1. Introduction: The Office is Dead (20 min)
2. The Pandemic Shift: March 2020 (30 min)
3. The Great Resignation: Why People Quit (35 min)
4. Building Remote Culture: What Works (30 min)
5. The Hybrid Debate: Best of Both Worlds? (30 min)
6. The Future: What's Next for Work (25 min)
```

## Production Tips

### For Quick Solo Episodes
- Use bullet points, not full scripts
- Record in a quiet space
- Keep it conversational and authentic
- Don't over-edit - some imperfection is okay
- Batch record multiple episodes

### For Structured Interviews
- Research your guest thoroughly
- Prepare 10-15 questions (use 5-7)
- Listen actively and ask follow-ups
- Test audio quality before recording
- Send questions to guest beforehand (optional)
- Record locally (not just over internet) for better quality

### For Narrative Series
- Write full scripts before recording anything
- Plan the entire arc before starting
- Record extra material (you'll need it in editing)
- Invest in good microphone and recording space
- Use professional editing software
- Consider hiring audio engineer for mixing

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

❌ **Don't**: Wing it without any preparation
✅ **Do**: At least create a bullet-point outline

❌ **Don't**: Record in a noisy environment
✅ **Do**: Find a quiet space with minimal echo

❌ **Don't**: Forget to hit record (it happens!)
✅ **Do**: Do a test recording first

❌ **Don't**: Talk over your guest constantly
✅ **Do**: Let them finish, then respond

❌ **Don't**: Publish without show notes
✅ **Do**: Always include timestamps and links

❌ **Don't**: Forget to promote the episode
✅ **Do**: Create social media clips and posts

## Technical Considerations

### Recording Setup
- **Microphone**: USB or XLR microphone (not laptop mic)
- **Headphones**: Closed-back headphones to prevent bleed
- **Recording software**: Audacity (free), Adobe Audition, Logic Pro, GarageBand
- **Remote recording**: Riverside.fm, Zencastr, SquadCast for remote guests

### Audio Quality
- **Sample rate**: 44.1 kHz or 48 kHz
- **Bit depth**: 16-bit or 24-bit
- **Format**: WAV for recording, MP3 for distribution
- **Levels**: Peak around -6 dB to -3 dB, avoid clipping

### Editing Checklist
- [ ] Remove long pauses and filler words
- [ ] Cut out false starts and mistakes
- [ ] Add intro and outro music
- [ ] Balance audio levels between speakers
- [ ] Add transitions between segments
- [ ] Export at proper bitrate (128-192 kbps for MP3)
